A look into their Stories/Missions

Herit Farms (New York)

They specialize in Hyrdrophonics: a tremendous way to limit the carbon disaster tradition farming can pose. Specifically, Hydroponics is a type of horticulture and a subset of hydroculture which involves growing plants without soil, by using mineral nutrient solutions in an aqueous solvent.

Seed and Bean Chocolate (Pennsylvania)

Great chocolate needs great cocoa beans. Seed and Bean is active in the Cocoa Horizons Foundation that thrives in Côte d’Ivoire, Ghana and Tanzania; also, it focuses on two key pillars to address the challenges and achieve real progress: productivity and community.

Sharondoah Beekeepers (Vermont)

A family built upon the health of bees. Specifically, they have instituted the following: "To provide additional food sources for bees before and after almond bloom, we are planting flower pastures near or within almond orchards. Not only do those blooming plants support honey bees, they provide food sources for native bees as well."

Tonial Orchards (Massachusetts)

They are at the forefront of assuring their apple cultivation is done in the most sustainable manner as possible. In fact, apple cultivation faces a number of challenges. Increasing global competition has put the focus on lowering costs whilst further improving sensory quality and shelf-life. There is a need to reduce inputs such as water, fertiliser and labour, both to save costs and reduce environmentally-damaging emissions and pollution. T here is a continual battle with fungal, viral and bacterial diseases as well as insect pests. In the long term there is a need for new varieties able to withstand disease or more extreme conditions associated with climate change: all of this they are committed to being the best in class.
